Carbonnade à la Flamande
Oven-Braised Version

(Beef and Onions Braised in Beer)

A traditional Flemish beef stew made with caramelized onions and beer. Brown sugar and balsamic vinegar soften the bitterness of the beer and create a rich, comforting sauce.

--------------------------------------------------

Yield
6 servings

Prep Time
20 minutes

Cook Time
2½–3 hours

--------------------------------------------------

Equipment

- Heavy skillet
- 9–10 inch casserole or Dutch oven with lid

--------------------------------------------------

Ingredients

- 3 lb chuck roast or rump roast
- 4 Tbsp olive oil
- 3 Tbsp butter
- 1½ lb onions, thinly sliced (about 6 cups)
- 3 cloves garlic, mashed
- Salt and pepper
- 1 cup beef stock
- 3 cups light beer or ale
- 2 Tbsp light brown sugar
- 1 bouquet garni (4 parsley sprigs, 1 thyme sprig, 1 bay leaf tied together)
- 1½ Tbsp cornstarch
- 2 Tbsp balsamic vinegar

--------------------------------------------------

Instructions

1. Prepare the Beef

Cut the beef into slices about ½ inch thick and pat dry.

Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C).

Heat the olive oil in a heavy skillet over high heat. Brown the beef quickly in batches, one layer at a time. Transfer browned meat to a plate.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Cook the Onions

Reduce heat to medium.

Add the butter and sliced onions to the skillet. Cook slowly until deeply golden brown.

Stir in the garlic and season lightly with salt and pepper.

Meanwhile, warm the beef stock.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Assemble the Stew

Lightly oil the casserole or Dutch oven.

Build alternating layers of beef and onions, seasoning each meat layer lightly with salt and pepper.

Pour the warm beef stock into the skillet and scrape up all the browned bits. Pour this liquid into the casserole.

Tuck the bouquet garni into the center of the stew.

Mix the brown sugar into the beer and pour over the meat and onions.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Braise

Bring to a gentle boil on the stovetop.

Cover and cook in the lower third of the oven for 2½–3 hours, until the beef is fork tender.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Finish the Sauce

Remove the bouquet garni.

Drain the cooking liquid into a saucepan over low heat.

Mix the cornstarch with the balsamic vinegar and stir into the sauce. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring constantly, until thickened.

Return the sauce to the casserole and gently mix.

--------------------------------------------------

Serve

Serve with pommes frites, steamed potatoes, or mashed potatoes.

--------------------------------------------------

Nonna’s Wisdom

- Brown the meat quickly over high heat so it develops color without losing its juices.
- Allow the onions enough time to become deeply golden without burning.
- A thick sauce is essential to the final dish. Stir the cornstarch mixture thoroughly and allow the sauce enough time to fully thicken.
- There are many variations of this recipe. This version is inspired by memories of the stew served in Antwerp.
