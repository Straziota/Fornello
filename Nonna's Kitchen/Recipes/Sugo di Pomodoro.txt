Category: Sauces and Salsas

Prep time: 10 min
Cook time: 50 min
Yeld: 4 cups

Ingredients 
4 Tbsp Olive oil
2 Whole Onions (Chopped)
2 Garlic cloves (Minced)
2 lb Canned crushed tomatoes
1 Tbsp tomato paste (optional)
1 Cup Fresh basil leaves, cut in strips or simply torn in pieces
3 Tsp sea salt

Directions
Add olive oil to a wok and stir fry onions on medium fire till they are translucent, then add garlic and optional tomato paste, allowing all to cook for a few minutes. Total time about 10 minutes.

Add crushed tomatoes, bring to a boil, and cook partially covered on medium to low flame for about 50 minutes, stirring frequently.
Season with salt and add the basil leaves cooking for just a few minutes so they impart their aroma to the sauce.Correct seasoning if needed.

My Notes
This is the quintessential tomato sauce for any kind of pasta, though I prefer it with spaghetti . It is a simplified version of my own grandmother's recipe, which was based on the typical Italian stir fry trio: onions, carrots and celery. Slow cooking time imparts a deep rich flavor which can be enhanced by the optional use of tomato paste, according to your taste.  It is important that you use crushed tomatoes and the right proportion of oil to tomato in order to obtain a smooth, thick silky sauce , rather than soup like. If necessary you can add more olive oil throughout the process; experience will be your best guide.
