Panallets

Based on my friend Torcuata’s Recipe

Ingredients
125 g White Sugar
300 g Almonds (blanched)
2 Tsp Finely grated Lemon peel (yellow part only)
1/2 Tsp Pure almond essence
50 g Cooked peeled sweet potato
1 Egg white
50 g Pine nuts
1 Egg yolk + 1 tsp water


Directions

Making the dough

1.Grind sugar to fine powder, add grated lemon peel and blend thoroughly.
2.Grind almonds to fine powder, add the sugar lemon peel mixture and blend thoroughly.
3.Mash cooked sweet potato and blend with egg white to obtain a smooth paste.
4.Add sweet potato mixture to almond sugar mixture and mix throughly obtaining a compact dough.
5.Shape the dough into a ball, wrap in plastic film and store in refrigerator 12 hours or overnight. The longer it rests the more homogenous will be the dough.

Shaping the sweets

1.Preheat oven to 375 F (180C).
2.Prepare egg wash mixing egg yolk and water.
3.Shape the dough into a 1 ½ inch (4cm) diameter cylinder.
3.Cut 3/4” (2cm) portions to roll into 1 ½ (4 cm) balls and coat each ball with pine nuts. The easiest way to do this is to place pine nuts in a bowl and, with a swirling movement, roll each dough ball on the pine nuts so that the pine nuts stick and cover the entire surface .
4.Place balls on parchment paper or silicon mat on baking sheet and brush with egg wash

Baking

1.Bake on middle wrack for 10 to 20 minutes until cookies are lightly browned.
2.Remove from oven, let cool on baking sheet for 10 minutes, carefully lift each panallet and allow to rest on a cooling wrack.
Store in airtight tin.

My Notes
Typical Catalonian sweets similar to marzipan, traditionally baked around November 1st for All Saints Day. The dough is usually rolled in pine nuts before baking, but coconut or cocoa powder can be used as a variation. They are my very favorite sweets because of the marzipan content.
