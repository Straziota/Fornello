Carbonnade à la Flamande
Slow Cooker Version

(Beef and Onions Braised in Beer)

A traditional Flemish beef stew made with caramelized onions and beer. Slow cooking creates exceptionally tender beef and a rich, velvety sauce.

--------------------------------------------------

Yield
6 servings

Prep Time
20 minutes

Cook Time
2½–3 hours on LOW

--------------------------------------------------

Equipment

- Slow cooker
- Heavy skillet (if slow cooker does not include sauté function)

--------------------------------------------------

Ingredients

- 3 lb chuck roast or rump roast
- 4 Tbsp olive oil
- 3 Tbsp butter
- 1½ lb onions, thinly sliced (about 6 cups)
- 3 cloves garlic, mashed
- Salt and pepper
- 1 cup beef stock
- 3 cups light beer or ale
- 2 Tbsp light brown sugar
- 1 bouquet garni (4 parsley sprigs, 1 thyme sprig, 1 bay leaf tied together)
- 1½ Tbsp cornstarch
- 2 Tbsp balsamic vinegar

--------------------------------------------------

Instructions

1. Prepare the Beef

Cut the beef into slices about ½ inch thick and pat dry.

If your slow cooker has a sauté function, heat the olive oil directly in the insert. Otherwise, use a heavy skillet.

Brown the beef quickly in batches over high heat. Transfer browned meat to a plate.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Cook the Onions

Reduce heat to medium.

Add the butter and sliced onions. Cook slowly until deeply golden brown.

Stir in the garlic and season lightly with salt and pepper.

Meanwhile, warm the beef stock.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Assemble the Stew

Build alternating layers of beef and onions directly in the slow cooker, seasoning each meat layer lightly with salt and pepper.

If a skillet was used, pour the warm beef stock into the skillet and scrape up all the browned bits. Pour this liquid into the slow cooker.

Tuck the bouquet garni into the center of the stew.

Mix the brown sugar into the beer and pour over the meat and onions.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Slow Cook

Cover and cook on LOW for 2½–3 hours, until the beef is fork tender.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Finish the Sauce

Remove the bouquet garni.

Mix the cornstarch with the balsamic vinegar and stir directly into the slow cooker.

Cook for an additional 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce becomes thick and smooth.

--------------------------------------------------

Serve

Serve with pommes frites, steamed potatoes, or mashed potatoes.

--------------------------------------------------

Nonna’s Wisdom

- Brown the meat quickly over high heat so it develops color without losing its juices.
- Allow the onions enough time to become deeply golden without burning.
- A thick sauce is essential to the final dish. Stir the cornstarch mixture thoroughly and allow the sauce enough time to fully thicken.
- There are many variations of this recipe. This version is inspired by memories of the stew served in Antwerp.
